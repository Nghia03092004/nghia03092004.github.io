\section{Problem Statement}

Let $S(A)$ represent the sum of elements in set $A$ of size $n$. We call it a \emph{special sum set} if for any two non-empty disjoint subsets $B$ and $C$:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $S(B) \neq S(C)$
    \item If $|B| > |C|$ then $S(B) > S(C)$
\end{enumerate}

Assuming condition~2 is satisfied, we only need to verify condition~1 for pairs where $|B| = |C|$. For $n = 12$, how many such pairs need testing?

\section{Mathematical Analysis}

\subsection{Counting Equal-Size Disjoint Pairs}

For subsets $B, C$ with $|B| = |C| = k$, the total number of unordered disjoint pairs is:
\[
\binom{n}{2k} \cdot \frac{1}{2}\binom{2k}{k}
\]

\subsection{Pairs Guaranteed by the Ordering Condition}

When we interleave the sorted elements of $B$ and $C$, if the interleaving is \emph{non-crossing} (every $b_i$ and $c_i$ paired by rank satisfy a consistent inequality), the ordering condition already guarantees $S(B) \neq S(C)$.

The number of non-crossing interleavings of $k$ $B$-elements and $k$ $C$-elements is exactly the $k$-th Catalan number:
\[
C_k = \frac{1}{k+1}\binom{2k}{k}
\]

Thus the number of pairs guaranteed safe for a given $2k$-element subset is $C_k$, and over all such subsets:
\[
\text{Guaranteed pairs} = \binom{n}{2k} \cdot C_k
\]

\subsection{Pairs Requiring Testing}

\[
T = \sum_{k=2}^{\lfloor n/2 \rfloor} \binom{n}{2k} \left[ \frac{1}{2}\binom{2k}{k} - C_k \right]
\]

\subsection{Computation for $n = 12$}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}cccccc@{}}
\toprule
$k$ & $\binom{12}{2k}$ & $\frac{1}{2}\binom{2k}{k}$ & $C_k$ & Difference & Contribution \\
\midrule
2 & 495 & 3 & 2 & 1 & 495 \\
3 & 924 & 10 & 5 & 5 & 4620 \\
4 & 495 & 35 & 14 & 21 & 10395 \\
5 & 66 & 126 & 42 & 84 & 5544 \\
6 & 1 & 462 & 132 & 330 & 330 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\[
T = 495 + 4620 + 10395 + 5544 + 330 = \boxed{21384}
\]

\section{Complexity}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Time:} $O(n)$ --- one pass over valid values of $k$.
    \item \textbf{Space:} $O(1)$.
\end{itemize}

\section{Answer}

\[
\boxed{21384}
\]
