\section{Problem Statement}
Cards labeled 1 to N at positions $3^n \bmod (N+1)$. Merge into ordered stack with minimum total drag distance $G(N)$. Given $G(6)=8$, $G(16)=47$. Find $G(976)$.

\section{Mathematical Analysis}
\subsection*{Permutation Sorting by Block Moves}

The card positions form a permutation determined by powers of 3 modulo $N+1$. $G(N)$ is the minimum total distance to sort this permutation by moving consecutive subsequences.

\subsection*{Greedy / DP Approach}

The optimal strategy identifies the longest already-ordered subsequence and moves the remaining cards to their correct positions. This connects to the **longest increasing subsequence** (LIS) of the permutation.

\subsection*{Connection to Cyclic Structures}

Since the permutation is $\sigma(n) = 3^n \bmod (N+1)$, the cycle structure of this permutation (orbits of multiplication by 3 modulo $N+1$) determines the sorting difficulty.
