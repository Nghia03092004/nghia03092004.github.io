\section{Problem Statement}

Stack $n$ plates into $k$ non-empty piles of distinct sizes. Let $f(n,k)$ be the maximum possible smallest pile, $F(n)=\sum f(n,k)$ over valid $k$, and $S(N)=\sum_{n=1}^{N} F(n)$. Given $S(100)=12656$, find $S(10^{16})\bmod 10^9+7$.

\section{Mathematical Analysis}

$f(n,k) = \left\lfloor \frac{n - k(k-1)/2}{k} \right\rfloor$ when $n \ge k(k+1)/2$. Sum via number-theoretic techniques.

\section{Complexity Analysis}

The floor-sum approach runs in $O(\sqrt{N})$.
