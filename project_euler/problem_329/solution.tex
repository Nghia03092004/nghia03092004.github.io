\section{Problem Statement}
A frog sits on a uniformly chosen square from 1 to 500. It jumps left or right with equal probability (boundaries force direction). On each square it croaks ``P'' with probability $\frac{2}{3}$ if the square is prime, $\frac{1}{3}$ otherwise; and ``N'' with complementary probabilities.

Find the probability that the 15-croak sequence is ``PPPPNNPPPNPPNPN'', as a reduced fraction.

\section{Approach}

\subsection{Dynamic Programming}
Let $dp[t][i]$ be the probability of producing the first $t+1$ letters and being on square $i$ after step $t$.

Initialize for step 0:
\[
dp[0][s] = \frac{1}{500} \cdot P(\text{croak}_0 \mid s), \quad s = 1, \ldots, 500
\]
where $P(\text{croak}_0 \mid s) = \frac{2}{3}$ if the croak matches the prime status, $\frac{1}{3}$ otherwise.

Transition for $t \geq 1$:
\[
dp[t][j] = P(\text{croak}_t \mid j) \cdot \sum_{i \sim j} P(\text{jump } i \to j) \cdot dp[t-1][i]
\]

\subsection{Result}
\[
\Pr(\text{sequence}) = \sum_{i=1}^{500} dp[14][i]
\]

Using exact rational arithmetic, the answer simplifies to:
\[
\frac{199740353}{29386561536000}
\]

\section{Answer}

\[
\boxed{199740353/29386561536000}
\]
