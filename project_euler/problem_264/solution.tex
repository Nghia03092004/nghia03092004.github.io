\section{Problem Statement}

Find all triangles $ABC$ with integer coordinate vertices on a circle centered at the origin (circumcenter $O=(0,0)$), with orthocenter at $H=(5,0)$ and perimeter $\le 10^5$. Compute the sum of their perimeters to 4~decimal places.

\section{Key Relation}

For a triangle inscribed in a circle centered at~$O$:
\[
\vec{OH} = \vec{OA}+\vec{OB}+\vec{OC}.
\]
Since $O$ is the origin and $H=(5,0)$: $A+B+C = (5,0)$.

\section{Algorithm}

All three vertices lie on $x^2+y^2 = n$ for some positive integer~$n$. Given $A=(a_1,a_2)$, the condition $C = (5{-}a_1{-}b_1,\, {-}a_2{-}b_2)$ on the same circle yields the linear constraint
\[
(2a_1{-}10)\,b_1 + 2a_2\,b_2 = 10a_1 - 25 - n.
\]
Combined with $b_1^2+b_2^2=n$, this is a line--circle intersection with at most 2 solutions for~$B$.

\subsection{Discriminant}
The discriminant simplifies to $\Delta = 16\,a_2^2\,D$ where
\[
D = n\bigl((2a_1{-}10)^2 + 4a_2^2\bigr) - (10a_1{-}25{-}n)^2.
\]
$D$ must be a non-negative perfect square for integer solutions to exist.

\subsection{Enumeration}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Iterate $A=(a_1,a_2)$ with $a_1^2+a_2^2 \le R_{\max}^2$ ($R_{\max}=20000$).
\item For each $A$, check if $D\ge 0$ is a perfect square.
\item Solve for $B$, verify $C$ on circle, non-degeneracy, perimeter $\le 10^5$.
\item Divide total by 6 (each unordered triple counted $3!=6$ times).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Results}

There are 155 valid triangles.

\section{Answer}

\[
\boxed{2816417.1055}
\]
