\section{Problem Statement}
Let $a_n$ be the largest real root of the polynomial $g(x) = x^3 - 2^n \cdot x^2 + n$.

Find $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{30} \lfloor a_n^{987654321} \rfloor \pmod{10^8}$.

\section{Solution}

\subsection{Perturbation of the Root}
For the cubic $g(x) = x^3 - 2^n x^2 + n$, the sum of roots equals $2^n$ (by Vieta's formulas).
The largest root $a_n$ satisfies $a_n \approx 2^n$ for large $n$. Writing $a_n = 2^n - \epsilon_n$
and substituting:
\[
(2^n - \epsilon_n)^3 - 2^n(2^n - \epsilon_n)^2 + n = 0
\]

Expanding to leading order in $\epsilon_n$:
\[
\epsilon_n \approx \frac{n}{2^{2n}}
\]

\subsection{Large Power Computation}
We compute $a_n^{987654321}$. Let $k = 987654321$. Then:
\[
a_n^k = \left(2^n - \frac{n}{2^{2n}} + O(n^2 / 2^{5n})\right)^k
\]
\[
= 2^{nk}\left(1 - \frac{n}{2^{3n}} + \cdots\right)^k
\]
\[
\approx 2^{nk} \exp\left(-\frac{kn}{2^{3n}}\right)
\]

For $n \geq 2$, the correction $kn/2^{3n}$ is extremely small, so the result is just below
$2^{nk}$, giving $\lfloor a_n^k \rfloor = 2^{nk} - 1$.

For $n = 1$, the root must be handled with more care since the perturbation approximation
is less accurate.

\subsection{Modular Arithmetic}
We compute $\lfloor a_n^k \rfloor \pmod{10^8}$ using fast modular exponentiation for
$2^{nk} \pmod{10^8}$ and subtract 1 as appropriate, then sum over $n = 1, \ldots, 30$.

\subsection{Result}
\[
\boxed{28010159}
\]

\section{Answer}

\[
\boxed{28010159}
\]
