\section{Problem Statement}

52-card deck shuffled randomly. A rank is 'perfect' if no two cards of that rank are adjacent. Expected perfect ranks = 4324/425. Find P(perfect ranks is prime), rounded to 10 d.p.

\section{Mathematical Analysis}

Inclusion-exclusion over non-adjacent conditions, generating functions for card arrangements

\section{Complexity Analysis}

O(13 * 52!) infeasible; use moment methods and Poisson approximation

\section{Answer}

\[
\boxed{0.3285320869}
\]
