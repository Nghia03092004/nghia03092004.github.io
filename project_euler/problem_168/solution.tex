\section{Problem Statement}
Find the sum of the last 5 digits of all integers $n$, $10 \le n < 10^{100}$, such that moving the last digit to the front yields a multiple of $n$.

\section{Mathematical Analysis}

Let $n$ be a $d$-digit number ($d \ge 2$). Write $n = 10q + r$ where $r$ is the last digit. The rotated number is:
\[
m = r \cdot 10^{d-1} + q
\]
We require $m = kn$ for some positive integer $k$.

\subsection{Digit-by-Digit Construction}
Write $n = a_1 a_2 \cdots a_d$. Then $m = a_d a_1 a_2 \cdots a_{d-1}$ and $m = k \cdot n$.

Consider the multiplication $k \cdot n$ digit by digit from right to left. Let $c_j$ be the carry into position $j$:
\begin{align}
k \cdot a_d + c_d &\equiv a_{d-1} \pmod{10}, \quad c_{d-1} = \lfloor (k \cdot a_d + c_d)/10 \rfloor \\
k \cdot a_{d-1} + c_{d-1} &\equiv a_{d-2} \pmod{10}, \quad c_{d-2} = \lfloor (k \cdot a_{d-1} + c_{d-1})/10 \rfloor \\
&\vdots \\
k \cdot a_1 + c_1 &\equiv a_d \pmod{10}, \quad \text{and final carry} = 0
\end{align}

Starting with $a_d = r$ and carry $c = 0$, we iterate:
\[
a_{d-1} = (k \cdot r + 0) \bmod 10, \quad c = \lfloor (k \cdot r)/10 \rfloor
\]
and continue. After $d$ steps, we need the generated digit to equal $r$ and the carry to be 0. This happens at cycle lengths dividing some period.

\subsection{Algorithm}
For each $k \in \{1,\ldots,9\}$ and $r \in \{1,\ldots,9\}$, simulate the digit generation. The state is $(a_i, c_i)$ which cycles with period at most 90. For each valid cycle length $d \in [2, 99]$, compute the last 5 digits of $n$ and add to the running sum.
