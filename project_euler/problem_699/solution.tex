\section{Problem Statement}

A positive integer $n$ is a triffle number if $\sigma(n)/n$ in lowest terms has denominator a power of 3. Let $T(N)$ be the sum of triffle numbers at most $N$. Given $T(100)=270$ and $T(10^6)=26089287$, determine $T(N)$ for large $N$.

\section{Mathematical Analysis}

Multiplicative structure: $\sigma(n)/n = \prod (p^{a+1}-1)/((p-1)p^a)$. The denominator is $3^k$ iff specific prime conditions hold.

\section{Complexity Analysis}

A sieve over the denominators of $\sigma(n)/n$ runs in $O(N)$.
