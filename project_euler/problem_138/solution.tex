\section{Problem Statement}
Consider an isosceles triangle with base $b$, equal legs of length $L$, and height $h$.
We require $|b - h| = 1$ (i.e., $h = b \pm 1$) and $L$ to be a positive integer.
Find $\sum L$ for the 12 smallest such triangles.

\section{Derivation}

\subsection{Pythagorean Relation}
The height splits the triangle into two right triangles:
\[
L^2 = h^2 + \left(\frac{b}{2}\right)^2
\]

With $h = b \pm 1$:
\[
L^2 = (b \pm 1)^2 + \frac{b^2}{4}
\]

Setting $b = 2m$ (we need $b$ even for $b/2$ to contribute cleanly):
\[
L^2 = (2m \pm 1)^2 + m^2 = 5m^2 \pm 4m + 1
\]

\subsection{Pell Equation}
Multiplying by 5:
\[
5L^2 = 25m^2 \pm 20m + 5 = (5m \pm 2)^2 + 1
\]

Let $X = 5m \pm 2$:
\[
X^2 - 5L^2 = -1
\]

This is a generalized Pell equation.

\subsection{Solutions}
The fundamental solution is $(X, L) = (2, 1)$. The general solution satisfies:
\[
(X_n + L_n\sqrt{5}) = (2 + \sqrt{5})^{2n-1}
\]

Extracting valid $L$ values (those giving positive integer $m$ and $b$), the sequence is:
\[
L_1 = 17, \quad L_2 = 305, \quad L_{n+1} = 18L_n - L_{n-1}
\]

\subsection{Verification}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c|r|r|r|r}
$n$ & $L$ & $m$ & $b = 2m$ & $h$ \\ \hline
1 & 17 & 8 & 16 & 15 \\
2 & 305 & 136 & 272 & 273 \\
3 & 5473 & 2448 & 4896 & 4895 \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Check $n=1$: $17^2 = 289 = 225 + 64 = 15^2 + 8^2$. $|16 - 15| = 1$. \checkmark

\item \textbf{Time:} $O(k)$ for $k$ terms via recurrence.
\item \textbf{Space:} $O(1)$.
\end{itemize}
