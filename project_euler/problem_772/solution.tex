\section{Problem Statement}
A $k$-bounded partition uses parts $\le k$. It's balanceable if splittable into two equal-sum halves. $f(k)$ = smallest $N$ where ALL $k$-bounded partitions of $N$ are balanceable. $f(3)=12$. Find $f(10^8) \bmod 10^9+7$.

\section{Analysis}
The critical value $f(k)$ grows roughly as $k^2/2$. For large $k$, the worst-case partition (hardest to balance) consists of many copies of $k$ and $k-1$.
