\section{Problem Statement}

Compute $F(N)=\sum_{i=2}^{N} S(i!)$ where $S(n)=\sum_{d\mid n} 2^{\omega(d)}$ and $\omega(d)$ counts distinct prime factors. Given $F(10)=4821$, find $F(10^7)\bmod 1{,}000{,}000{,}087$.

\section{Mathematical Analysis}

Dirichlet convolution: $2^{\omega(n)} = \mu^2 * 1$, followed by a multiplicative-function sieve.

\section{Complexity Analysis}

The sieve runs in $O(N \log \log N)$.
