\section{Problem Statement}

A square is drawn around a circle. The blue shaded region between them is called the ``L-section.'' A diagonal line from the bottom left to the top right creates a ``concave triangle.''

When $n$ circles are placed horizontally with a bounding rectangle and a diagonal line from bottom-left to top-right, find the least $n$ for which the concave triangle occupies less than $0.1\%$ of the L-section.

\section{Mathematical Derivation}

\subsection{Setup}
Consider $n$ unit circles ($r = 1$) placed horizontally. The bounding rectangle has dimensions $2n \times 2$. The diagonal line has equation $y = \frac{x}{n}$.

The L-section (one quarter) has area:
\[
A_L = 1 - \frac{\pi}{4}
\]

\subsection{Intersection Point}
The leftmost circle has center $(1, 1)$ and equation $(x-1)^2 + (y-1)^2 = 1$.

Setting $y = x/n$:
\[
(x - 1)^2 + \left(\frac{x}{n} - 1\right)^2 = 1
\]

Let $s = 1/n$. Expanding:
\[
(1 + s^2)x^2 - 2(1 + s)x + 1 = 0
\]

The relevant (smaller) root is:
\[
x_0 = \frac{(1+s) - \sqrt{2s}}{1+s^2}
\]

\subsection{Concave Triangle Area}
The area has two components:

\textbf{Part 1:} Triangle under the line from $0$ to $x_0$:
\[
A_1 = \frac{x_0^2 s}{2}
\]

\textbf{Part 2:} Area between circle bottom and $x$-axis from $x_0$ to $1$. The bottom of the circle is $y = 1 - \sqrt{1 - (x-1)^2}$.

\[
A_2 = \int_{x_0}^{1} \left(1 - \sqrt{1 - (x-1)^2}\right) dx
\]

Substituting $u = x - 1$:
\[
A_2 = \int_{x_0 - 1}^{0} \left(1 - \sqrt{1 - u^2}\right) du
\]
\[
= \left[u - \frac{u\sqrt{1 - u^2}}{2} - \frac{\arcsin u}{2}\right]_{x_0 - 1}^{0}
\]

\subsection{Ratio Condition}
We seek the least $n$ such that:
\[
\frac{A_1 + A_2}{1 - \pi/4} < 0.001
\]

\section{Result}
The answer is $\boxed{2240}$.

\section{Answer}

\[
\boxed{2240}
\]
