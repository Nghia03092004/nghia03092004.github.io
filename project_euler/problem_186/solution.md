# Problem 186: Connectedness of a Network

## Problem Statement

Here are the records from a busy telephone system with one million users:

The telephone number of the caller and the called number in record $n$ are generated by a "Lagged Fibonacci Generator":

For $1 \leq k \leq 55$: $S_k = [100003 - 200003k + 300007k^3] \pmod{10^6}$

For $k > 55$: $S_k = [S_{k-24} + S_{k-55}] \pmod{10^6}$

The caller in the $n$-th record is $S_{2n-1}$ and the called is $S_{2n}$.

If the caller equals the called number, no connection is made. Otherwise, the two subscribers are connected.

How many successful calls are needed so that 99% of the subscribers (including the PM, subscriber number 524287) are connected to the PM?

## Mathematical Foundation

**Definition 1.** A *Disjoint Set Union* (DSU) data structure maintains a partition of $\{0, 1, \ldots, n-1\}$ into disjoint sets, supporting two operations: $\textsc{Find}(x)$ returns the representative of $x$'s set, and $\textsc{Union}(x, y)$ merges the sets containing $x$ and $y$.

**Theorem 1 (Tarjan, 1975).** A DSU with path compression and union by rank supports any intermixed sequence of $m$ $\textsc{Find}$ and $n$ $\textsc{Union}$ operations in $O((m + n)\,\alpha(n))$ time, where $\alpha$ is the inverse Ackermann function.

**Proof.** This is the classical result of Tarjan (1975). Path compression flattens the tree during each $\textsc{Find}$, and union by rank ensures the tree depth is $O(\log n)$ before compression. The amortized analysis via Ackermann functions shows that the per-operation cost is $O(\alpha(n))$, which is effectively constant for all practical values of $n$. $\square$

**Theorem 2 (Correctness of the LFG).** The Lagged Fibonacci Generator with the given initial conditions and recurrence produces a deterministic sequence $S_1, S_2, \ldots$ of pseudo-random values in $\{0, 1, \ldots, 999999\}$. Each pair $(S_{2n-1}, S_{2n})$ determines the $n$-th call.

**Proof.** The initial conditions $S_k = (100003 - 200003k + 300007k^3) \bmod 10^6$ for $1 \leq k \leq 55$ are well-defined. The recurrence $S_k = (S_{k-24} + S_{k-55}) \bmod 10^6$ for $k > 55$ is a linear recurrence modulo $10^6$ with fixed lag parameters 24 and 55 (the same lags used in Knuth's subtractive generator). The sequence is deterministic given the initial 55 values. $\square$

**Lemma 1 (Erdos-Renyi Scaling).** In the random graph $G(n, M)$ on $n$ vertices with $M$ random edges, a giant component of size $\geq (1-\varepsilon)n$ emerges when $M = \Theta(n \log(1/\varepsilon))$. For $n = 10^6$ and $\varepsilon = 0.01$, this predicts $M \approx$ a few million calls, consistent with the answer.

**Proof.** By the Erdos-Renyi theory, the critical threshold for the giant component is $M \sim n/2$. Above this threshold, the giant component grows, and the time to absorb all but $\varepsilon n$ vertices scales as $\Theta(n \log(1/\varepsilon))$ (cf. Bollobas, *Random Graphs*, 2001). The answer of 2,325,629 is consistent with $n \log(100) \approx 4.6 \times 10^6$ accounting for constant factors and the specific pseudorandom sequence. $\square$
