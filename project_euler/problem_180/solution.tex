\section{Problem Statement}

Define $f = f_1 \cdot f_2 \cdot f_3$ where each factor involves $x^m + y^m - z^m$ for various exponents $m$ related to parameter $n$. Golden triples $(x,y,z)$ have all coordinates as proper fractions $a/b$ with $1 \leq a < b \leq k$ and satisfy $f(x,y,z) = 0$ for some $|n| \leq k$.

Find $p + q$ where $S(35) = p/q$ (reduced), and $S(k)$ is the sum of all distinct $s = x + y + z$ values.

\section{Solution}

\subsection{Key Equations}

The zeros of $f$ reduce to $x^m + y^m = z^m$ for various integers $m$.

By Fermat's Last Theorem (Wiles, 1995), there are no positive rational solutions for $|m| \geq 3$.

The case $m = 0$ gives $1 + 1 = 1$, which is false.

The surviving cases are:
\begin{align}
m = 1: &\quad z = x + y \\
m = 2: &\quad z = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \\
m = -1: &\quad z = \frac{xy}{x+y} \\
m = -2: &\quad z = \frac{xy}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}}
\end{align}

\subsection{Validity of $z$}

For each pair $(x, y)$ of proper fractions $a/b$ with $1 \leq a < b \leq 35$, compute $z$ in each case and verify:
\begin{itemize}
\item $z > 0$ and $z < 1$ (proper fraction)
\item $z$ in reduced form has denominator $\leq 35$
\item For $m = \pm 2$: $x^2 + y^2$ must be a perfect square of a rational
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Summation}

Collect all distinct values of $s = x + y + z$ and compute:
\[
S(35) = \sum_{\text{distinct } s} s = \frac{p}{q}
\]

\subsection{Result}

\[
p + q = \boxed{285196020571078987}
\]

\section{Answer}

\[
\boxed{285196020571078987}
\]
