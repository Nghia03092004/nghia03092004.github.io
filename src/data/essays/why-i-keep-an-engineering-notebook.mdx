An engineering notebook does two jobs at once.

First, it lowers the cost of coming back to a problem after a week or a month.

Second, it makes the work legible while it is still in motion. That matters more than people admit. A lot of wasted effort comes from not knowing what the current bottleneck actually is.

## What I do not put in it

I avoid turning notes into polished documentation too early.

That usually leads to one of two failures:

- I stop writing because the bar feels too high.
- I write too much and bury the useful part.

Instead, I aim for notes that are accurate, skimmable, and compressible later.

## The standard I care about

The real test is simple:

If I reopen the note after two months, can I restart the work in ten minutes instead of an hour?

If yes, the note did its job.
